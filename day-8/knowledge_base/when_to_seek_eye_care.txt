When to Seek Urgent Eye Care

Many eye conditions can be managed with routine appointments, but some symptoms require prompt or emergency assessment. Delays in seeking care for certain eye conditions can result in permanent, irreversible vision loss. Knowing which symptoms warrant urgent attention is an important part of protecting eye health.

Symptoms that require same-day or emergency assessment include sudden loss of vision in one or both eyes, sudden appearance of a large number of new floaters (dark spots, threads, or cobweb-like shapes in the field of vision), flashes of light particularly in the periphery of vision, a shadow or curtain moving across the visual field, sudden eye pain especially when accompanied by nausea or vomiting, chemical or physical injury to the eye, and a white or cloudy appearance in the pupil of a child's eye. These symptoms may indicate serious conditions such as retinal detachment, acute angle-closure glaucoma, or retinoblastoma in children, all of which require rapid treatment to preserve vision.

Red eye with significant pain, sensitivity to light, and decreased vision — rather than the mild irritation typical of common conjunctivitis — also requires urgent professional assessment. Similarly, an eye that has been struck, cut, or had a foreign body enter it should be assessed promptly. The eye should not be rubbed or pressed if penetrating injury is suspected.

For chemical injuries, immediate first aid is to irrigate the eye with large amounts of clean water for at least 15 to 20 minutes before seeking emergency care. Acid and alkali burns can cause severe and permanent damage, and rapid irrigation is one of the most important steps in limiting harm.

Routine but non-urgent symptoms — such as gradual blurring of vision, mild redness without pain, difficulty reading, or increased glare sensitivity — should still be assessed by an eye care professional within days to weeks rather than being ignored. Many common eye conditions respond well to treatment when identified early, but may cause lasting harm if left unaddressed. Anyone who is unsure whether their symptoms require urgent care should contact a healthcare professional for guidance.
